A geographer from Central Kalimantan.

I work at the intersection of land, forest, and data - currently as Planning and Mapping Unit Head at Nusantara Climate Initiative in Pulang Pisau, developing nature-based climate solutions across Kalimantan's forests and peatlands.

This blog is not about my work. It is where I write whatever crosses my mind - book reviews, personal reflections, thoughts on faith, and occasional observations on development and inequality in Indonesia. I have lived in Java and Sumatra, but Kalimantan is where I think clearly.

The writing on environment, geography, and carbon will come. For now, notes from every journey.
